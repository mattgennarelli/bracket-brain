📐 How This Works

Bracket Brain runs 10,000 Monte Carlo simulations of the entire tournament to generate win probabilities for every possible matchup and calculate each team's chances of advancing to each round.

The model blends two signals: tempo-adjusted efficiency metrics (adjusted offensive and defensive efficiency, tempo) which are the single best predictor of college basketball outcomes, weighted at 80%; and historical seed performance data from 1985-2025, weighted at 20%, which captures information embedded in the selection committee's decisions that pure stats may miss.

For each simulated game, the model predicts expected points for both teams based on the offensive/defensive efficiency matchup and expected pace, then adds realistic game-to-game variance (σ ≈ 11 points) to determine the winner. This means even heavy favorites lose sometimes, and the probabilities reflect that uncertainty.

Team stats sourced from Bart Torvik's T-Rank (barttorvik.com). Historical seed data compiled from NCAA tournament records 1985-2025.
